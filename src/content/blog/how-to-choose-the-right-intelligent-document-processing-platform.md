# How to Choose the Right Intelligent Document Processing Platform

Businesses evaluating intelligent document processing (IDP) platforms face a crowded market. Whether you are looking to replace a legacy tool, automate AP workflows for the first time, or find a solution that handles messy real-world documents, the selection criteria matter more than brand names.

This guide covers what to evaluate when choosing an IDP platform, with practical benchmarks and considerations for finance, operations, and IT teams.

## What Is Intelligent Document Processing?

Intelligent Document Processing uses AI, machine learning, and OCR to automate the extraction, understanding, and management of information from unstructured documents. Unlike basic OCR that simply converts images to text, IDP understands document structure and extracts data as structured key-value pairs ready for use in business systems.

A complete IDP platform combines optical character recognition for text extraction, machine learning for understanding document layouts, and natural language processing for interpreting context and meaning.

## Key Evaluation Criteria

### 1\. Pre-Processing Capabilities

Real-world documents are messy. Faded receipts, skewed scans, blurry photos, and mixed handwritten-printed text are the norm, not the exception.

**What to look for:** Automatic image enhancement, skew correction, noise removal, and quality assessment. Platforms with strong pre-processing handle poor quality inputs without requiring users to rescan documents.

FormX uses proprietary image pre-processing and OCR result post-processing to achieve 92% extraction accuracy, minimizing the need for human intervention.

### 2\. Pre-Built Extractors

Configuring templates for every document type is time-consuming and fragile - layouts change, vendors switch invoice formats, and new document types appear constantly.

**What to look for:** Ready-to-use extractors for common document types that work without template configuration. This dramatically reduces setup time and ongoing maintenance.

FormX includes pre-built extractors for invoices, receipts, bank statements, ID documents, and business certificates that work out of the box.

### 3\. Extraction Accuracy

Accuracy claims vary widely across vendors. The key is understanding what accuracy means in practice for your specific documents.

**What to look for:** Transparent accuracy metrics with clear definitions. Request an accuracy report on your actual documents rather than relying on marketing benchmarks.

FormX achieves 70%+ field accuracy across all documents. For good quality images, date, time, and amount fields achieve 90%+ accuracy. FormX offers accuracy reports based on your sample documents.

### 4\. Integration Architecture

How the platform connects to your existing systems determines whether automation actually works in practice.

**What to look for:** API-first architecture for developer teams, no-code connectors (Zapier, N8N) for business users, and flexible export formats (JSON, CSV) for compatibility with any system.

FormX offers API-first architecture with Zapier, N8N, and Google Workspace integrations. It can be easily integrated with different systems including accounting and ERP software.

### 5\. Processing Speed

Document processing speed affects both user experience and throughput capacity.

**What to look for:** Per-document processing time, not just batch throughput. For interactive workflows (customer-facing apps, real-time validation), sub-10-second processing is essential.

FormX typically processes receipts in 4-8 seconds, excluding upload time.

### 6\. Mobile Capture

For use cases involving field workers, customers, or anyone capturing documents with a phone, mobile capture quality determines extraction accuracy.

**What to look for:** Mobile SDKs with guided capture (document detection, blur detection, camera stabilization) rather than basic camera integration.

FormX's mobile SDK includes real-time document detection, live preview, blur detection, camera stabilization, and long receipt stitching.

### 7\. Security and Data Handling

Financial documents contain sensitive data. How the platform handles your documents matters as much as how accurately it reads them.

**What to look for:** No document storage (in-memory processing), encryption in transit, clear data retention policies, private deployment options, and explicit opt-in for any AI training on your data.

FormX processes documents in-memory and disposes them after extraction. All API calls are encrypted with HTTPS. FormX is hosted on GCP and Azure, with private cloud deployment available for enterprise plans. Documents are not used for AI model training unless explicitly requested.

### 8\. Pricing Transparency

IDP pricing models vary: per page, per document, monthly subscription, per field, or custom enterprise quotes. Hidden costs for features, integrations, or support add up quickly.

**What to look for:** Clear per-page or per-document pricing, free trial for testing, and scalable plans that grow with your volume.

## Common Reasons Teams Switch IDP Platforms

### Accuracy on Real Documents

Marketing accuracy claims and real-world accuracy on your specific documents are often different. Teams switch when their current tool underperforms on poor quality scans, handwritten text, or non-standard layouts.

### Integration Complexity

Legacy platforms often require complex middleware or custom development to connect with existing systems. API-first platforms with standard export formats simplify integration significantly.

### Template Maintenance

Template-based systems require ongoing configuration as vendor invoice formats change. Pre-built extractors that adapt to layout variations reduce this maintenance burden.

### Cost at Scale

Some platforms become prohibitively expensive at higher volumes, or charge separately for features that should be standard (pre-processing, multiple document types, API access).

### Mobile Requirements

Customer-facing use cases (loyalty programs, expense capture, field documentation) require mobile SDKs. Not all IDP platforms offer this.

## Evaluation Checklist

Use this checklist when evaluating IDP platforms:

\- Test on your actual documents (not just demo samples)

\- Verify accuracy on your specific document types and quality levels

\- Check integration options for your existing systems (API, Zapier, CSV export)

\- Understand the pricing model at your expected volume

\- Review security practices (document storage, encryption, data retention)

\- Evaluate mobile capture capabilities if needed

\- Ask about pre-built extractors vs template configuration

\- Understand the onboarding and support model
